Morgan J. Rees

PhD Candidate in Mathematics at the University of Kent, specializing in computational modeling of topological solitons and quantitative finance. My work focuses on numerical solutions for Black-Scholes and Heston PDEs for option pricing.

About Me

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Kent, with a thesis on "The Solitonic Waltz: Abelian Higgs Vortex Dynamics." My research involves advanced computational modeling and numerical methods, including parallel computing with OpenMP. I have a strong background in quantitative finance, with expertise in option pricing, portfolio optimization, and algorithmic trading, developed through professional development courses and personal projects in Python and C++.

Education: PhD in Mathematics (2021-2025, viva pending), MSc Mathematics (First Class Hons, 2020-2021), BSc Mathematics (First Class Hons, 2017-2020), all at the University of Kent.
